Delivery Phases — 13 Components

P1 Critical

Foundation — Project Structure & Core SC Module Scoping

7 components
The architectural backbone. Establishes the project container, permissions model, context bar, and scopes all core SC modules to a project. Nothing else can be built without these.
1
Project Module Overview Page — grid/hierarchy view of all projects, stat strip, contract value, status filter
2
Project Creation & Setup — create form, team assignment, SC group/site mapping, status lifecycle
3
Project Home Screen (Hub) — persistent context bar, project switcher, tab navigation strip
4
Overview Tab — KPI stat cards, Today on Site panel, Recent Activity feed, quick action shortcuts
5
Inspections Tab — project-scoped inspection register, pass rate stat strip, overdue highlighting
6
Actions Tab — project-scoped action register, priority/status indicators, overdue count
7
Issues Tab — project-scoped issue register, severity colour coding (Critical/High/Medium/Low)
⚠️
Blocking decision: Project-to-Group/Site mapping must be locked before Phase 1 begins — it determines every API call in the module. Options: (a) 1 Project = 1 SC Site, (b) Project owns Sites, (c) Project is a Group. Engineering to confirm before any P1 code is written.
P2 High

Core Construction — Plans & Drawings, Programme Gantt, Org Hierarchy

5 components
The features that differentiate SafetyCulture Construct from base SC. Plans & Drawings and Programme Gantt are the two most-requested capabilities from construction customers.
8
Documents Tab — construction-categorised document management: SWMS, ITP, Plans, Contracts, Certs
9
Plans & Drawings Register — drawing number, discipline (A/S/C/M/E), revision, status, markup count badges
10
Drawing Viewer & Markup Tools — PDF viewer, annotations, raise SC action/issue directly from a markup pin
11
Programme / Schedule Gantt — phase-based Gantt, critical path, hold points linked to SC inspections, At Risk flags, weather overlays
12
Org Hierarchy View — visual org tree (Company → Region → Project) for portfolio-level leadership visibility
P3 Medium

Advanced — Crew & Contractor Management

1 component
Extends the module with live crew visibility, induction tracking, cert expiry alerts, and subcontractor management. Depends on Phase 1 and 2 being fully stable.
13
People & Crew Management — live crew roster, induction status, certification expiry alerts, contractor company tracking
📋
Next phase (after v1.0): Workers Passport (cross-site identity & compliance record for workers across multiple contractors), Permits to Work, and Schedule Change Management are scoped for the phase following full platform delivery.
✅
Reinstated to scope: AI Schedule Intelligence (critical path analysis, weather overlays, at-risk flagging) and Mobile Field Experience — drawings on phone — are confirmed in scope and ship as part of the 6-month delivery plan.

Signals & ICP Intelligence

What the data tells us about who buys, what they need, and what the Construct go-to-market should prioritise.

🎯
Champion shift: The traditional SC construction champion is the HSE Manager. The Construct modules shift the primary champion to the Project Manager / Site Foreman. This changes the outreach script, demo flow, and onboarding playbook — engineers and PMs should understand this when building personas.
🏗️

The Sweet Spot is 20–500 Employees

Tier 2/3 residential homebuilders, smaller commercial builders, and civil SMBs have enough project complexity (multiple active jobs, drawing registers, crew on site) to need Construct — but not the procurement friction of Tier 1 enterprises. Large construction companies have existing ERP/project management integrations that make quick wins much harder.

💳

1,633 ICP Accounts Already Have a Credit Card on File

Of the 1,975 ICP accounts in the existing SC base, 1,633 have a card on file. These are Motion 1 priority targets — they can be activated via an in-app free trial with no outbound sales motion at all. A 25% conversion rate yields ~494 accounts and A$3.0M incremental ARR from self-serve alone.

📐

Plans & Drawings is the #1 Feature Request

The most-cited gap when construction customers explain why they use a separate tool alongside SC. The drawing register with revision tracking, combined with the ability to raise an SC action directly from a markup pin on a drawing, is a significant workflow unlock. It replaces email chains and tools like Bluebeam for most site teams.

📅

Schedule Visibility is the Decision-Maker Pain

While PMs and foremen engage with the day-to-day project tools, the Org Hierarchy view and Programme Gantt are what get sign-off from construction directors and business owners. Being able to see all active projects — their schedule status, what's at risk, what's behind — in a single view is the executive hook.

🌍

AMER is the Largest Add-on Opportunity (A$15.4M)

Despite ANZ being SC's home market, AMER holds A$15.4M of the A$24.2M add-on total. This is driven by a larger ICP pool (~110K businesses vs ~11.5K in ANZ), higher avg MRR/seat, and a US construction market where project management software is more embedded in procurement culture. The AMER motion is predominantly M2 net-new.

Sub-Vertical Breakdown ICP Priority Targets

🏠 Tier 2/3 Homebuilders
Multiple active residential projects, high drawing volume, critical path pressure from land settlements. SC already strong on pre-start toolbox talks. Construct adds the project management layer and drawing control the office team needs.
🏢 Smaller Commercial
Fit-out and refurb specialists. Higher drawing complexity, tighter client-facing reporting requirements. Plans & Drawings + markup directly replaces their current PDF workflow — usually email chains or Bluebeam — in a product they're already paying for.
🛤️ Civil SMB
Roads, infrastructure, utilities. High hold point and inspection density, weather-sensitive scheduling. The Programme Gantt with weather overlays is the key differentiator. Crew management is critical due to subcontractor complexity.
